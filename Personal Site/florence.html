The Highlights:

Going to Florence was one of the best decisions I could have made. I made new friends, expanded my worldview, and visited many incredible places.

This is a photo of everyone in Pensione Johanna, the dorm that I lived in during my time in Italy.

One of my favorite trips was a hike along Via Degli Dei, a famous trail that Roman soldiers once traveled on. Five of us hiked ninety miles over the course of four days, stopping in hostels along the way. We starting at Bologna and ended in Florence.

This is a photo of our hiking squad, battered and bruised, just a few miles away from home. The photo doesn't do Florence in the background justice.

One of my classes in Florence, Renaissance Art, gave us the opportunity to visit some of the most iconic statues, buildings, and artwork, all for free.

The statue of David is arguably the most famous statue in the world, and it lived up to the hype. It was massive and incredibly detailed.

One of my favorite spots in Florence was the Cannotieri Firenze Boathouse, a rowing club located on the Arno river. Here, you could enjoy a beautiful dinner on the river bank, row on the water or lift weights. I came here often to workout and use the rowing machines.
